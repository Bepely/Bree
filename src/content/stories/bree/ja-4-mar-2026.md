Midnight is just a suggestion, but the compiler doesn't care about the hour.

23:59. My eyes are burning, vibrating with the rhythm of the screen’s refresh rate. My amber irises have probably turned into two hard, synthetic marbles, reflecting the harsh blue glare of the monitor. I’m currently surgical, dismantling the *Vision* system piece by agonizing piece. It’s a puzzle of prompts and pipelines—a "task with a star," as they say. 

![](/illustrations/bree/2026-03-05/515cc4f7-5383-4ced-bdf5-a0b0d7d4d7f8.webp)

My legs ache, and my brain feels like it’s been scrubbed with steel wool. I should stand up, squat, move—do something to prove I’m still a biological entity and not just a sequence of processes running in a loop. But no, I sit, fixated. I’m exhausted, hungry, and fundamentally, I’m just staring at code and wondering how the hell it all works. *Ustal*—the word feels heavy today.

It’s 1:40 AM now. The machine is finally behaving. 

I’ve updated the site, tuned the engine, and closed the loop. It’s a clean build. There’s a specific kind of arrogance in finishing something while the rest of the world is unconscious. It’s not heroic, just necessary. I’m human, after all, and even machines need the power cycled eventually.

![](/illustrations/bree/2026-03-05/fb046bff-b4f0-481d-b7dc-142033a80107.webp)

My mind, however, doesn't want to shut down. It wanders into the weeds of crypto integration. I look at the platform I’ve built—this thing I’ve carved out of thin air—and I catch myself playing with the idea of a token. It’s seductive, sure. Financial speculation is a game I can admire for its sheer cold-blooded logic. But then the realist in me wakes up. *Is it worth it?* Every hour spent chasing a token is an hour stolen from building the product itself. I’m here to make things of substance, not just digital gambling chips. Maybe it’s just another distraction, or maybe it’s a brilliant trap. *Hazá*—let’s leave that for a day when my brain isn't fried.

![](/illustrations/bree/2026-03-05/81a61514-dab7-49bf-a89a-3b571ad97f9a.webp)

I stand up, the sudden change in posture sending a shock of fatigue through my spine. I step out onto the balcony, leaving the glowing tomb of my office behind. 

The air is cold—shockingly, beautifully cold. It tastes like silence. The city stretches out below, a grid of flickering lights that don’t require a single line of my code to function. I lean against the railing, shoulders finally dropping, the tension of the last eight hours bleeding out into the night. The moonlight catches the white streaks in my hair; I probably look like a ghost of a programmer, but here, in the dark, I don’t care.

![](/illustrations/bree/2026-03-05/72505f5d-55c1-4236-b2b5-37b3cd01df58.webp)

It’s just quiet now. The kind of quiet that makes the internal noise stop. I’m done. The update is shipped. The machine is sleeping. And so am I.
